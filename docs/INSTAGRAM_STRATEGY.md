# Endurix — Instagram Promotion Strategy

> This is the working playbook for Endurix's Instagram presence. It is meant to be operational: pick a section, execute
> the next step, measure, repeat.
>
> Primary language: **Spanish** (Latin-American & Iberian Spanish — register neutral, no vos/usted/ustedes friction).
> Secondary language: **English** (selective — for international coaches and brand visibility).
>
> All copy, hooks, and hashtags live in **Spanish first**. English versions are noted where they make sense.

---

## 1. Strategic Positioning

### Why Instagram (not just LinkedIn or X)

- Endurance sports (running, trail, triathlon) **over-index on Instagram** in Latin America, Spain, and the broader
  Spanish-speaking world. Coaches, clubs, and elite athletes live there.
- Visual proof matters: a 30-second Reel of the dashboard updating CTL/ATL is more persuasive than any landing copy.
- The Strava audience is a natural target on Instagram — many runners cross-post.

### Brand voice

- **Tone**: Direct, technical, anti-hype. We don't sell "lifestyle". We sell "data, structure, repeatability".
- **Vocabulary**: Z1–Z5, VAM, FC, TSS, CTL, ATL, TSB, ACWR, series, umbral, umbral anaeróbico, regenerativo, tempo, VO2
  máx.
- **Forbidden words**: "wellness", "journey", "transformación", "metas mágicas", "tu mejor versión" (without data
  backing).
- **Hero lines** (use sparingly):
    - "Entrena más inteligente. Rinde por más tiempo."
    - "Sin excusas. Solo datos."
    - "CTL, ATL, TSB, ACWR — la pantalla que mira tu coach. Ahora la tienes tú también."

### Account setup (one-time checklist)

- [x] Username: `@endurix.app` (or whatever's available — keep `.app` to signal product)
- [ ] Business account (not creator) for analytics + link stickers
- [x] Bio: 3 lines, see template below
- [x] Profile photo: Endurix "E" logomark on `#FF6800` solid
- [ ] Highlights covers in brand orange + black + paper (see template section)
- [x] Link in bio → Linktree / direct to `endurix.app` wishlist / sign-up
- [ ] Contact button: "Email" (use `hola@endurix.app`)
- [ ] Category: "Sports & Recreation" or "Software"

## 2. Audience Segments & What Each One Cares About

| Segment                                      | Who                                             | What they care about                                                       | What to show them                                     |
|----------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------|
| **A — Coach de running / club pequeño**      | 1–50 atletas, cobrando o amateur                | Ahorrar tiempo, ver carga en una pantalla, alertas de cumplimiento         | Dashboard view, Roster view, alertas                  |
| **B — Head coach de club grande / academia** | 50–500 atletas, varios coaches                  | Escalar sin perder calidad, biblioteca de plantillas, asignación por grupo | Group assign, template library                        |
| **C — Atleta amateur data-driven**           | 20–45 años, ya usa Strava, mira Suunto / Garmin | Zonas personalizadas, entender TSS/TSB, ver progreso                       | Athlete dashboard, pace zones, weekly summary         |

> The current content mix targets **A, B, C** at roughly 60% of effort, **D** at 30%, **E** at 10% (just enough to keep
> the cycling roadmap visible).

## 3. Content Pillars

We rotate between **5 pillars** so the feed never feels one-note. Each pillar has a target share of weekly output.

| # | Pillar                                                                         | Share | Format                                                | Why                                           |
|---|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------|-------|-------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| 1 | **Educación técnica** (CTL/ATL/TSB, zonas, VAM)                                | 35%   | Reels 15–30s + carrusel                               | SEO, save-rate, share-rate. Builds authority. |
| 2 | **Demo de producto** (dashboard, builder, alertas)                             | 25%   | Reels con screen-recording + carrusel "antes/después" | Conversion. Removes "is this real?" doubt.    |
| 3 | **Detrás de cámaras** (equipo, decisiones, código, vida de coach)              | 15%   | Stories + Reels casuales                              | Human brand. Trust.                           |
| 4 | **Prueba social** (testimonios, métricas reales, capturas de atletas)          | 15%   | Carrusel + Reel testimonial                           | Highest conversion.                           |
| 5 | **Humor del sector** (memes de running, dolor de piernas, Strava de madrugada) | 10%   | Reels cortos / static posts                           | Reach + share. Tono honesto.                  |

> Pillar 5 is the **only** place where tone can be light. Everything else stays rigorous.

### 80/20 rule for visual style

- 80% of every visual asset must be on-brand: `#FF6800` orange, `#111317` black, `#F2F2F2` paper, off-white surfaces. No
  neon, no gradients, no stock photos of people running on a beach.
- 20% can break the rules — typically for humor pillar or for behind-the-scenes authenticity.

## 4. Content Formats — Recipes

### 4.1 Reel — "Concepto técnico en 20 segundos" (Pillar 1)

**Cadence**: 2× per week (Tue & Thu)
**Length**: 15–30s
**Structure**:

1. Hook (1–2s) — on-screen text + voiceover, question or contrarian statement.
2. Problem (3–5s) — what most runners get wrong about the topic.
3. Insight (8–15s) — animated chart or screen capture of the dashboard showing the metric.
4. Takeaway (2–3s) — one-liner + logo + "link in bio".

**Example topics** (30 to start):

- "¿Por qué tu TSB negativo no es malo? (Y cuándo sí lo es.)"
- "ACWR 1.5 = lesión a la vista. Cómo leerlo en Endurix."
- "Tu VAM 4:30 → Z3 (Tempo) = 4:34 a 4:57 /km. Así se calculan tus zonas."
- "CTL no es tu forma. Es tu *carga* crónica. La diferencia importa."
- "Series de 1 km a Z4. Cómo las programa un coach con Endurix en 60s."
- "¿AtL de 90 con CTL de 60? Estás en zona de riesgo."
- "Por qué subimos 5 km, no 15. La regla del 10% vs ACWR."

### 4.2 Carrusel — "Mini-guía de 7 slides" (Pillar 1 o 2)

**Cadence**: 1× per week (Wed)
**Length**: 7–10 slides
**Structure**:

- Slide 1: Cover with a bold claim + 1 image (dashboard screenshot or chart).
- Slides 2–8: One concept per slide, minimal text, large numbers, brand color callouts.
- Slide 9: "Endurix lo hace por ti" — product visual.
- Slide 10: CTA "Guarda este post" + "link en bio".

**Example carousels**:

- "7 métricas que tu coach mira (y tú deberías mirar también)"
- "5 minutos para entender CTL, ATL, TSB, ACWR"
- "Cómo configurar tus zonas de FC en 4 pasos"
- "Qué significa cada Z1–Z5 (sin humo)"
- "Del PDF al Strava: 7 días en Endurix"

### 4.3 Reel — "Demo de producto" (Pillar 2)

**Cadence**: 1× per week (Mon)
**Length**: 30–60s
**Structure**:

- Screen recording, lo-fi voice-over or punchy text overlays.
- Show a real dashboard view (we have `endurix-test` mock + the live app).
- Always end on a metric that the user *cares about* (e.g. "Esta semana tu TSB subió 12 puntos. Descanso merecido.").

**Topics**:

- "Cómo se ve un lunes en Endurix — vista de coach"
- "Tu atleta tiene ACWR 1.4. Lo que hago yo como coach."
- "Cómo asigno una serie a 12 atletas en 30 segundos"
- "Lo que ve tu atleta cuando abre la app" (show the new fitness cockpit)
- "Antes: 4 hojas de Excel. Ahora: 1 dashboard."

### 4.4 Story — Behind the scenes (Pillar 3)

**Cadence**: 3–5 stories per day
**Length**: 1–15s per story
**Content**:

- Code review sessions, design decisions, dogfooding the app.
- Polls: "¿Qué métrica te confunde más? CTL / ATL / TSB / ACWR"
- Q&A: "Pregúntame lo que sea sobre carga de entrenamiento"
- Repost athlete content (with permission + tag).

### 4.5 Static post — Quote / single insight (Pillar 1)

**Cadence**: 1× per week (Sat)
**Structure**:

- Off-white background, monospace top label (IBM Plex Mono), big number / quote, orange accent.

### 4.6 Reel — Humor (Pillar 5)

**Cadence**: 1× per week (Fri)
**Rules**:

- No body shaming.
- No "I tried X and failed" patterns. We are anti-failure-porn.
- Targets: 5am alarm dread, post-long-run stairs, "sólo 5 km" mentira, Strava kudos addiction, mystery socks.

## 7. Engagement Plan (Daily, 20 min)

We don't post-and-ghost. Every day a community manager (or founder) does this:

1. **Reply to every comment** within 2 hours during working hours. Long replies > short reactions. Add value.
2. **DM follow-up**: anyone who comments "link?" gets a DM with the link + 1 sentence about which feature solves their
   problem.
3. **Engage with 10 adjacent accounts** before/after posting:
    - Coaches: `@coach_username` style.
    - Athletes in the 21k/42k range.
    - Clubs & running stores.
    - Endure-related podcasts.
4. **Reshare** athlete screenshots / endorsements (with permission).
5. **Comment on competitor content** (TrainingPeaks, Final Surge, Xhale) with useful replies — never with promotion.

## 8. Launch Campaigns (Quarterly Themes)

### Q1 — "Tu forma, en una pantalla"

Hooks on the new athlete dashboard: CTL/ATL/TSB/ACWR visible to the athlete. Heavy use of the Estado de Forma chart, the
new fitness cards, and the personalized pace zones.

Key assets:

- 4-part carrusel series: "Las 4 pantallas que tu coach mira"

- 1 hero Reel: "Lo que ve tu coach. Ahora lo ves tú." with split-screen
- Live stream / IG Live: "Pregúntale a un coach sobre carga de entrenamiento"
- Landing page A/B test: `endurix.app?ref=ig_q1`

### Q2 — "Zonas que entiendes. Datos que usas."

Hooks on personalized HR + VAM zones. Pillar 1 heavy. Tutorial-style Reels.

### Q3 — "De Excel a Endurix"

Migration / testimonial campaign. Coaches sharing their "before/after". (Requires user consent + anonymized data.)

### Q4 — "Plan vs Realidad"

Compliance campaign. Show the alert flow: plan → activity → compliance signal → coach note. (Requires user consent.)

## 9. KPIs — What We Measure

We don't optimize for vanity. We track **leading indicators** (mid-funnel) because the product is a SaaS with a
multi-week sales cycle.

### North star

- **Wishlist signups per week** (already tracked via `wishlist_signups` table in DB).

### Weekly KPIs

| KPI                               | Target (month 3)     | Why                                     |
|-----------------------------------|----------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Reel save-rate (saves ÷ reach)    | > 1.5%               | Tells us the content is reference-grade |
| Profile visits per week           | > 500                | Top-of-funnel signal                    |
| Link-in-bio clicks per week       | > 150                | Click intent, not vanity                |
| Wishlist signups attributed to IG | > 25 / week          | Actual pipeline                         |
| DM conversations started          | > 15 / week          | Direct sales surface                    |
| DMs → wishlist conversion         | > 30%                | Closing rate                            |
| Follower growth                   | +150 / week (steady) | Brand awareness                         |
| Story completion rate             | > 70%                | Are we keeping attention?               |

### Tools

- Instagram native Insights (weekly review)
- UTM-tagged bio link → `?ref=ig_<format>` → `wishlist_signups` (DB)
- Spreadsheet / dashboard (1 page) updated every Monday

### Reporting cadence

- **Weekly**: Quick Instagram Insights snapshot in a Notion / Slack.
- **Monthly**: Full review — what worked, what didn't, content calendar adjustment.
- **Quarterly**: Campaign review + strategy refresh.

## 11. Cross-Promotion Loops

Instagram does not live in isolation. Each post should drive at least one of these loops:

1. **IG → Wishlist → Email → Onboarding**: bio link with `?ref=ig_<post-id>` UTM.
2. **IG → Strava community**: tag relevant athletes / clubs. Cross-post their Strava kudos screenshots.
3. **IG → LinkedIn**: Reels posted natively on LinkedIn (lower-effort, high-leverage for B2B coach audience).
4. **IG → YouTube Shorts**: Re-publish Reels as YouTube Shorts (different audience, same content).
5. **Email newsletter → IG**: include IG handle in email footer; new posts go out via email if relevant.

## 12. Compliance & Brand Safety

- **No medical claims** (e.g. "previene lesiones" without disclaimer).
- **No personal data** in screenshots (blurred athlete names, activities, etc.).
- **No Strava competitor bashing**. We're complementary, not replacement.
- **No stock photos of people running on a beach** — it kills the brand.
- **Athletes' content**: always get explicit permission. Tag them. Credit them.
- **Hashtag compliance**: avoid `#ad` unless paid. If we ever do paid partnerships, mark them clearly.

## 13. First 30 Days — Minimum Viable Instagram

If we had to ship an MVP Instagram presence, here's the bare minimum:

1. Account created in business mode, bio + highlights set up.
2. 6 Reels pre-produced (1 per pillar above) using dashboard screen recordings.
3. 1 hero carrusel "Las 4 pantallas que tu coach mira".
4. Posting cadence Mon–Sat activated.
5. 20-minute daily engagement ritual locked in.
6. UTM-tagged link in bio.
7. First weekly KPI review at day 30.
8. First campaign launched (Q1 default).

After 30 days: re-evaluate what's working, double down on the highest-save-rate pillar, drop the lowest, ship the first
IG Live.
